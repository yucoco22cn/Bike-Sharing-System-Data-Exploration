Effects of customer behaviour on the number of bike trips¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, I wanted to look at the customer behavior of the bike sharing system of the Bay area that could be used to figure out when the bikes are high in demand (during the day/weekday/month). Moreover which age range uses the service the most and if the service is mostly used by members or casual riders.The main focus was on the following characters: start time, trip duration, customer type, customer age.

Dataset Overview¶

There are 1863721 entries in the dataset with 15 features. Besides the features mentioned above, other features like trip ID, trip start station, customer gender are included.Data points were removed from the analysis due to inconsistencies or missing information, 436882 entires are used in the final analysis.

Distribution of Trip Durations¶

Looking at the histogram, we can see that trip durations are no longer than 30 min (1800 sec) and usually last 6 to 15 min. It looks like people use the system for commuting, they trips are usually short probably due to the closeness of their homes to workplace/school.

Weekday and Hourly Usage by Customer Type¶

Customers use the bike sharing system more often on weekends:

weekdays: most bike rides are around 8-9am and 5-6pm with the peak on Fridays around 5pm
weekends: most bike rides are between 10am - 8pm with the peak on Saturdays around 2pm

Subscribers use the bike sharing system mainly on weekdays:

weekdays: most bike rides are around 8-9am and 5-6pm with the peak on Tuesdays around 8am
weekends: bikes are still rented but there is a significant drop in numbers of rented bikes throughout the entire weekends.

Monthly Usage by Customer Type and Customer Age¶

The trend is similar for the age groups: for customers, the highest demand is around summertime, reaching its peak in July; for subscribers, the highest demand is from May till October, reaching its peak in October.
For customers, young people (18-29) use the system more frequently than the mid-ages (30-39) during the high demand season (Jun-Oct). While for the subscriber, the data is vice versa, mid-age use the system more than young people.

Weekday Usage by Customer Type and Customer Age¶

The usage trend is more evident in the weekly graph. For the customer, young people (18-29) are more likely to use the system on the weekend compared with other age groups. For the subscriber, mid-age people use the system more to commute.
